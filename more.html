Cybersecurity Awareness Starts With You

Cybersecurity awareness is about recognising danger before damage is done. It is the knowledge and daily habits that protect data systems and people from cyber threats.

The Threats You Are Most Likely to Face

Cyber attacks are no longer rare or complex. Most succeed because they exploit human behaviour rather than technology.

Phishing Scams
Fraudulent emails or websites designed to steal passwords financial details or personal information by pretending to be trusted sources.

Malware
Malicious software that damages systems steals data or allows unauthorised access. Often spread through downloads or infected links.

Social Engineering
Psychological manipulation that pressures people into revealing confidential information or bypassing security rules.

Denial of Service Attacks
Attempts to overwhelm systems so legitimate users cannot access services. These attacks target availability rather than data.

Simple Habits That Stop Most Attacks

Good cyber hygiene reduces risk dramatically. These habits take minutes to apply but prevent the majority of attacks.

Use Strong Passwords
Create unique complex passwords for each account and store them securely using a trusted password manager.

Keep Software Updated
Updates close security gaps that attackers actively exploit.

Think Before You Click
Verify links attachments and unexpected messages especially when urgency is involved.

Enable Two Factor Authentication
Even if a password is compromised this extra step blocks unauthorised access.
